Chapter One

The Programme Philosophy

Tomorrowland builds kingdoms. Burning Man builds cities. Zamna builds sanctuaries. EDC builds spectacle. Zungu builds an island.

Every zone operates simultaneously. At any given hour, 2,000 adults are choosing their own experience — whether that is deep in the forest with a paintball rifle, suspended in a hammock listening to an ambient installation, sweating in a cold plunge at dawn, or dancing on the main stage at 3am.

Nobody is ever waiting. Nobody is ever bored. Nobody is ever doing the same thing as everyone else.

The island is the programme. The programme is the island.

Simultaneity
Every zone runs in parallel. No queues, no bottlenecks, no single point of congregation except by choice.
Discovery Over Announcement
Some experiences are not on the programme. You find them by walking into the forest at midnight, or by asking the right person.
Adults Only, Always
18+ throughout. No softening. The bar programme, the wellness spaces, the night activations — all designed for adults who know what they want.
Local First, Global Stage
Every zone has a Portland Parish operator at its core. Activities are not imported — they are rooted here and elevated for the world.

Chapter Two

Zone Architecture

Eight permanent zones, active simultaneously across all four days. Each zone has its own name, its own operating hours, its own sensory identity.

🎯
THE AMBUSH
Forest Combat Zone · Paintball Jamaica

Deep in the Navy Island forest. Managed by Paintball Jamaica. Six distinct terrain formats across 4 acres. The only zone where you earn something at the end.

  • Classic woodsball — 6 sessions daily (max 30/session)
  • Night sessions with UV phosphorescent paint — 10pm & midnight
  • Team tournament — Day 3 bracket, prizes + trophy ceremony
  • Speedball arena — smaller court, faster pace, spectator viewing
  • Sniper solo challenge — 1v1 timed elimination
  • Signature cocktail at the Armoury Bar post-session
Daytime & Night · Ticketed Sessions · 18+
🌊
THE COVE
Beach & Water Activation

The western beach. Where the Caribbean sea and the festival intersect. Every water activity curated by local Portland guides. No motorised watercraft near the reef.

  • Dawn paddle: SUP at 6am, guided, max 12 people
  • Reef snorkel — marine biologist-led, 90 mins, ecology brief
  • Deep sea fishing charter — private half-day, 8 max
  • Kayak exploration — self-guided, mangrove detour option
  • Sunset catamaran — live soundsystem, rum punch, 2hr nightly
  • Floating DJ set — anchored platform 50m offshore, afternoons
  • Cliff jump point — guided only, safety assessment, waiver
6am – 11pm Daily · Reef-Safe Zone
🌿
THE SANCTUM
Wellness, Recovery & Ritual

Adults-only wellness precinct. No music above 60 decibels. No phones after the entrance gate. Designed for those who need to recover, reset, or simply be still.

  • Sunrise yoga & breathwork — 5:45am, open air, guided
  • Cold plunge pool — spring-fed, open 5am to midnight
  • Hot/cold contrast therapy — Nordic protocol
  • Massage pavilion — Swedish, deep tissue, sports recovery
  • Sound bath chamber — crystal bowls, gongs, 3× daily
  • IV hydration lounge — electrolytes, B12, Vitamin C
  • Guided ceremony — Day 3 only, pre-register, 8 max
5am – Midnight · No Phones · 18+
🏛️
THE YARD
Culture, Talk & Participation

The cultural anchor of the island. Soundsystem history, Patois workshops, vinyl market, rum masterclass, live debate, open mic. Where the thesis is spoken aloud.

  • Soundsystem history sessions — panels and live demonstration
  • Patois workshop — twice daily, interactive, beginner-friendly
  • Vinyl market — local selectors bring crates
  • Rum masterclass — Jamaican distillery partnership
  • Film screening — 11pm nightly, Jamaican cinema archive
  • Poetry & spoken word — 2–3:30pm daily, closes before stages
8am – 3:30pm Cultural · Evening Film
🎨
THE STUDIO
Art, Making & Creation

A working creative space. Portland Parish artist-in-residence. Festival-goers contribute to a live mural. Darkroom, ceramics, screen printing, indigo dyeing — all open.

  • Live mural — Portland artist, festival-goer contribution Day 3
  • Ceramics wheel — open sessions, instructor available
  • Screen printing — custom t-shirt, limited edition designs
  • Darkroom — analogue photography, guided sessions
  • Indigo dyeing — traditional Jamaican technique
  • Jewellery-making — beads, wire, silver-work workshop
7am – 3pm Daily · Walk-In Welcome
🍽️
THE MARKET
Food, Drink & Portland Vendors

All Portland Parish vendors. Boston Bay jerk, seafood raw bar, Blue Mountain coffee, rum bar, Jamaican patisserie. The only 24-hour zone on the island.

  • Boston Bay jerk — wood fire, pork and chicken, open all day
  • Seafood raw bar — fresh daily, lobster, conch, fish
  • Blue Mountain coffee service — from 5:30am
  • Chef demonstration — one Portland chef per day, open kitchen
  • Rum bar — curated Jamaican rums, bar manager from Portland
  • Non-alcoholic programme — Jamaican nutritionist-designed
5:30am – 3am Daily · All Local
🌲
THE TRAIL
Forest Immersion & Hidden Activations

A 2km walking route through the Navy Island forest. Not all of what you find on it is on the map. Some installations are found, not announced — a Burning Man principle.

  • Guided forest walk — ecology and island history, 7am, max 15
  • Hammock forest — 40 hammocks, first-come, quiet zone
  • Hidden ambient installation — generative sound, deep in forest
  • Secret bar — unmarked, discoverable only by full trail walk
  • Aerial silks platform — bookable 30-min sessions
  • Night trail — lit with lanterns only, guided midnight walk
Dawn to Midnight · Mostly Free
🌙
THE PIER
Intimate Night Stage · No Announced Lineup

200-capacity. The walk to it is part of it. No lineup posted. You arrive to discover who is playing. Zamna's intimacy, transposed to the Caribbean. Opens at midnight.

  • 200 maximum — managed by door count, no wristband upgrade
  • No lineup published — identity revealed only at the door
  • Sunrise set — final DJ plays until 8am on Day 4
  • Private bar — Pier guests only, rum-focused, no menu
  • No phones policy — enforced at entrance with lens stickers
  • Acoustic intermissions — between DJ sets, live only
Midnight – Dawn · 200 Cap · Discovery Only

Chapter Four

The Sanctum

Not a spa. A recovery and ritual space for adults at a 4-night festival. Designed for people who dance until dawn and need to survive four days without their body breaking.

No phones beyond the gate. No music above ambient. No performance. The Sanctum operates independently of the festival's energy — a counterweight, not a feature.

Chapter Six

Commitments & Responsibilities

Nine commitments. All contractual, not aspirational. Documented and published post-event.

01
Zero Single-Use Plastics
No plastic bottles, cups, or packaging anywhere on the island. Reusable cup provided at entry. Compost stations at every food node. NEPA compliance documented and submitted post-event.
02
Reef Protection Protocol
All water activities guided or reef-aware. Reef-safe sunscreen provided at entry and enforced at The Cove. Marine biologist on-site all four days. Post-event reef assessment filed with NEPA.
03
Portland Parish Employment First
Every zone coordinator is Portland-based. Every food vendor is local. Every construction crew drawn from Portland Parish. This is contractual. Employment numbers documented and published post-event.
04
Bilingual Wayfinding
Every sign, map, and zone name in English and Jamaican Patois. Patois is the first language of the island. The festival speaks it. Workshops run twice daily in The Yard.
05
Carbon Contribution Line
A fixed contribution is built into every ticket price — not a voluntary add-on. Directed to Portland Parish reforestation and marine conservation. Amount and allocation published transparently pre-sale.
06
Non-Alcoholic Programme
Designed by a Jamaican nutritionist and treated as a full programme — not a concession. Cold-press juices, herbal teas, coconut water cocktails, sorrel and ginger blends. Named, curated, not an afterthought.
07
Harm Reduction
On-site harm reduction team, discreet and non-punitive. Testing kits at The Sanctum. Water, bananas, and electrolytes free at every stage. No judgement. No escalation unless medical.
08
Quiet Zone
The forest hammock area is a designated quiet zone — no amplified music, no group noise, no exceptions. For introverts, the overwhelmed, people who need to exist at a festival without performing it.
09
Island Restoration
Full demobilisation begins within 24 hours of last ferry. All portable infrastructure removed. Environmental compliance documented. NEPA post-event report submitted as condition of Year 2 clearance.
